// Research Areas

Mapping the Boundary
of our Heliosphere

🌊
Pickup Ion Dynamics at Shocks
Investigating the acceleration and heating of interstellar pickup ions at distant interplanetary shocks, planetary bow shocks, and the heliospheric termination shock using high-cadence in-situ measurements from New Horizons/SWAP.
⚡
Energetic Neutral Atom Observations
Using high-resolution ENA flux maps from IBEX-Hi to understand the time-dependent evolution of the heliosphere, including the strength and geometry of the termination shock across multiple sky directions.
🛰️
IMAP / SWAPI Instrument Science
Leading the development of Level 3 science algorithms for the Solar Wind and Pickup Ion (SWAPI) instrument — a top-hat electrostatic analyzer measuring solar wind protons, alpha particles, and interstellar pickup ions at the Sun-Earth L1 point.
🌌
Solar Wind–LISM Interaction
Analyzing global ENA models and 3D MHD heliosphere simulations to extract the compression ratio and global structure of the termination shock from IBEX observations, and probing coronal hole evolution via ENA spectral signatures.

2025
High-resolution Observations of Pickup-ion-mediated Shocks to 60 au
Bishwas L. Shrestha et al. — ApJ
Analysis of 14 distant interplanetary shocks observed by the SWAP instrument aboard New Horizons from ~52–60 au, with 30-minute pickup ion cadence, characterizing fast-mode shocks in the outer heliosphere and the role of pickup ion pressure.

2024
Suprathermal H⁺ Pickup Ion Tails in the Outer Heliosphere
Bishwas L. Shrestha et al. — ApJ 960, 35
Detailed analysis of five distant interplanetary shocks observed by New Horizons/SWAP at 23.71–36.75 au, demonstrating for the first time how downstream H⁺ PUI tail number density varies with shock compression ratio.
→ scholar.google.com
2025
The Solar Wind and Pickup Ion (SWAPI) Instrument on NASA's IMAP
B. L. Shrestha, J. Teifert, S. E. Weidner et al. — Space Science Reviews
Full instrument paper for SWAPI: a top-hat electrostatic analyzer measuring energy-per-charge distributions of solar wind protons (H⁺), alpha particles (He²⁺), and interstellar PUIs across 0.1–20 keV/q at the Sun-Earth L1 Lagrange point.
→ Springer · Space Sci. Rev.
2023
Tracking the Rapid Opening and Closing of Polar Coronal Holes through IBEX ENA Observations
Bishwas L. Shrestha et al. — ApJ 943, 34
Using IBEX ENA spectral index maps to trace latitudinal boundaries between fast and slow solar wind, revealing a rapid evolution of polar coronal holes in 2012–2014 with a ~2.5-year transit delay to the heliospheric boundary.
→ scholar.google.com
2021
Strength of the Termination Shock Inferred from Globally Distributed ENA Flux from IBEX
Bishwas L. Shrestha et al. — ApJS 254, 32
Quantitative comparison of observed and simulated inner heliosheath ENA fluxes using a 3D steady-state MHD heliosphere model with multi-Maxwellian proton distributions, estimating the termination shock compression ratio across multiple sky directions.
